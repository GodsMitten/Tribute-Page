Ernest Gary Gygax

"Father of role-playing games"

Here's a time line of Gary Gygax's life:

  • July 27, 1938 - was born in Chicago, Illinois
  • 1946 - due to fracas with local gang of boys, family moved to Gary's mother's family home in Lake Geneva, Wisconsin
  • 1956 - Gary dropped out of high school in his junior year and joined the Marines.
  • 1957 - was diagnosed with walking pneumonia, he received a medical discharge and moved back home to Chicago with his mother
  • 1958 - he proposed to Mary Jo Powell and soon they got married
  • 1962 - Gygax got a job as an insurance underwriter at Fireman's Fund Insurance Co. His family continued to grow, and after his third child was born, he decided to move his family back to Lake Geneva
  • 1966 - Gygax, along with Bill Speer and Scott Duncan, co-founded International Fderation of Wargamers (IFW), which brought together several existing wargame clubs, promoted the interests of fans and organized forums for them
  • 1967 - Gygax organized a 20-person gaming meet in the basement of his home; this event would later be referred to as "Gen Con 0"
  • 1969 - in August, at the Gen Con 2, Gygax met Dave Arneson, future co-creator of Dungeons & Dragons
  • 1971 - Gary co-created Chainmail with Jeff Perrin, a desktop wargame using miniatures on a medieval theme.
  • 1973 - with Don Kaye as a partner, Gary Gygax founded Tactical Studies Rules, later known as TSR, Inc.
  • 1974 - the first commercial version of D&D was released by TSR in January as a boxed set
  • 1977 - TSR Hobbies released a completely new and complex version of D&D, Advanced Dungeons & Dragons (AD&D)
  • 1985 - due to a conflict with the new owner of TSR, Lorraine Williams, Gygax left the company he had created. company and continued to develop role-playing games independently
  • 1986 - after leaving TSR, Gygax created the game company New Infinities Productions, Inc.
  • 1990 - Gygax decided to create an entirely new RPG called The Carpenter Project - more complex and "rule heavy" than his original and relatively simple D&D system
  • 1995 - Gary began work on a new computer role-playing game called Lejendary Adventures but he was not able to successfully release it, so Gygax decided to publish it as a tabletop game instead
  • March 4, 2008 - died in Lake Geneva, Wisconsin
